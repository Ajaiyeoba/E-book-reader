The Author

Wole Soyinka

Style: Art, Fiction

Soyinka is a playwright, a novelist, and a poet. His books include a memoir called The Man Died: Prison Notes about his time in solitary confinement, and a novel called The Interpreters about university grads in a new Nigeria. He was awarded the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1986; the first one awarded to an African.
